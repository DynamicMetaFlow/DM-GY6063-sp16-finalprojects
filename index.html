If you were a ...

This book is a collection of sketches created by my Spring 2016 graduate Creative Coding class at NYU's Integrated Digital Media department. Each sketch uses the creative coding library p5.js.

Though I call this a book, it isn't meant to be read or experienced in a linear way. Many of the sketches are not only visul but interactive, requiring you as the reader, the user, to move your mouse or click on the page.

What makes this interactive book, experience, site, whatever you call it, so unique is that for most if not all of the students, this semester has been their very first introduction to computer programming. Furthermore, all of the code that comprises each sketch is available and remix-able by clicking on the big green Remix button on the top right of the window. I hope that by sharing this code and encouraging others to build from it, we will proliferate a burgeoning generation of artists, designers, tinkerers, and thinkers- those who approach coding not for its utility but rather for its expressiveness of creativity.

When planning the book, the students and I decided to set a theme through consensus- If you were a ... For some students the nuance of, if you versus if I is incredibly personal. To others, it's an invitation for political cynicism or a darker glimpse of data visualized.

Thank you to all of my students for your patience and focus, for tackling bugs, and embracing the idiosyncrasies of coding.

--Kevin Siwoff, May 2016.
